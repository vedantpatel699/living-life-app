# Living Life — Shift Work Adaptation

You work nights at Amazon: **1:20 AM to 11:30 AM, 2-4 days a week.** Standard fitness plans assume daytime schedules. Yours can't. This doc is the playbook for adapting to shift work without breaking your fat loss + fitness goals.

---

## Your day, flipped

A typical Amazon-shift day looks like this:

| Time | Activity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **12:30 AM** | Wake-up snack / coffee | Light. Caffeine OK now (start of your "morning"). |
| **1:20 AM – 11:30 AM** | Amazon shift | ~10 hours active. This IS your cardio. |
| **11:30 AM – 12:30 PM** | Decompress, light meal | Don't overeat. Body wants to crash. |
| **12:30 PM – 8:30 PM** | **SLEEP** | 8 hours. This is your "night." Dark room, white noise, no phone. |
| **8:30 PM – 9:00 PM** | Wake up, hydrate | Like a daytime person's morning. |
| **9:00 PM – 9:30 PM** | Pre-workout meal | Carbs + a bit of protein. ~300-400 kcal. |
| **9:30 PM – 10:30 PM** | **WORKOUT** | Body is rested. Hours before shift. This is your sweet spot. |
| **10:30 PM – 12:00 AM** | Dinner + prep for shift | Bigger meal — your "dinner." |
| **12:30 AM** | Out the door | Coffee for the road. |

An **off day** is closer to a normal schedule. Use those for catching up on social life, longer/harder workouts, batch cooking.

## The three core principles for shift work fitness

### 1. Sleep is the lever. Everything else amplifies or weakens it.

Day-sleep is ~30% lower quality than night-sleep for most people. Combined with your fat-loss target, this is the #1 risk factor. Protocol:

- **Blackout curtains** are non-negotiable. Cheap on Amazon ($30-50).
- **White noise** or earplugs to mask daytime sounds. Bedroom must feel like 3 AM.
- **Same sleep window every shift day** — even on off days, try to keep your sleep ±2 hours of your shift schedule to avoid permanent jetlag.
- **No caffeine within 6 hours of bed** — for you, no coffee after ~6:30 AM (mid-shift cutoff).
- **Cool the room** — 65-68°F (18-20°C). Lower than you think.
- **Heavy meal NOT right before sleep** — ideally 2+ hours before. So eat your post-shift "breakfast" by 11:45, then crash around 12:30.

### 2. Workouts go BEFORE the shift, not after.

Working out post-shift means:
- Your cortisol is already elevated from 10 hours of physical work
- Adding more cortisol from training delays sleep onset by ~45 min
- Body temperature stays high — sleep is shallower
- Recovery from the workout overlaps with the worst sleep of your week

Working out PRE-shift (~9-10 PM) means:
- Body is rested from full 8-hour sleep block
- Pre-workout meal can be 30-60 min before
- Post-workout cool-down → dinner → shift = natural rhythm
- Performance is actually GOOD because you've been awake and moving for an hour

**Workout duration on shift days:** keep to **30-40 min**. Don't go for 60-min epic sessions before a 10-hour shift. Save those for off-days.

### 3. Amazon shift IS your cardio. Adjust accordingly.

10 hours of walking, lifting, scanning at Amazon is roughly **400-700 active calories**. Your Garmin/Health Connect will capture this. Implications:

- **Don't add a long run on a shift day.** You've already done your cardio.
- **Strength sessions on shift days are fine** — different muscle groups, complementary.
- **Off days are when you do C25K / longer runs / Routine C.** That's where you push.

## The week, structured

Assuming a typical week with shift days Mon-Tue-Wed (adjust based on your actual schedule):

| Day | Shift? | Workout | Sleep | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Amazon | Strength @ 9:30 PM (30 min) | 12:30 PM – 8:30 PM | Routine A or upper body |
| **Tue** | Amazon | Rest (Amazon = cardio) | 12:30 PM – 8:30 PM | Recovery day. Light stretching only. |
| **Wed** | Amazon | Strength @ 9:30 PM (30 min) | 12:30 PM – 8:30 PM | Routine B or lower body |
| **Thu** | Off | C25K or longer run @ AM/PM | Normal night (11 PM – 7 AM) | First "real" sleep of the week — protect it |
| **Fri** | Off | Strength full session (45-60 min) | Normal night | Push intensity. Routine C. |
| **Sat** | Off | Long walk or C25K | Normal night | Active recovery + cardio |
| **Sun** | Off | REST | Normal night | Full recovery before the cycle restarts |

**Critical:** the Thursday after a 4-shift Mon-Thu pattern is brutal. Your circadian rhythm is wrecked. Don't plan anything cognitively demanding that morning. The first 24 hours after switching back are when ALL the deficit accumulates as fatigue. Eat protein, hydrate, sleep early.

## Meal timing — the rules

The classic "3 meals, 8pm dinner cutoff" rule from PLAN.md does NOT apply on shift days. Use these instead:

### Shift days

| Meal | Time | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Wake-up snack** | 12:30 AM | Coffee + a piece of fruit or 1 paratha. Light. |
| **Mid-shift meal** | 5:30 AM (your "lunch") | ~500-600 kcal. Dal-rice, paneer wrap, sandwich. Protein-anchored. |
| **Post-shift breakfast** | 12:00 PM (your "dinner before sleep") | ~400-500 kcal. Easily digestible. Not greasy/heavy. Dahi, fruit, eggs/paneer. |
| → SLEEP | 12:30 PM – 8:30 PM | |
| **Pre-workout meal** | 9:00 PM | ~300-400 kcal. Banana + peanut butter. Or oats. |
| **Dinner** | 10:30 PM | Main meal of the cycle. ~700 kcal. Veg + dal + roti + paneer/yogurt. |
| **TOTAL** | | ~2,000 kcal across 5 small meals |

### Off days

Standard daytime pattern from PLAN.md.

### Common shift-work eating mistakes

- **Snacking through the entire shift** → adds 300-600 kcal you don't track
- **Big greasy "breakfast" right before sleep** → indigestion, terrible sleep, fat storage
- **Skipping meals on shift days** → blood sugar crash mid-shift, performance tank
- **Caffeine at 6 AM** → ruins daytime sleep (caffeine half-life 5-6h)

## Supplement timing on shift days

The supplements doc assumes daytime. Updated for your schedule:

| Supplement | Shift day timing | Off day timing |
|---|---|---|
| **Creatine 5g** | With wake-up snack (12:30 AM) | Anytime |
| **Whey protein** | Post-workout (10:00 PM) OR with mid-shift meal | Post-workout / breakfast |
| **Vitamin D3** | With mid-shift meal (5:30 AM, has fat) | With breakfast |
| **B12 (methylcobalamin)** | Anytime — with wake-up | Anytime |
| **Algal omega-3** | With mid-shift meal | With breakfast |
| **Caffeine** | First 4 hours of shift only (1:30-5:30 AM). NO LATER. | Pre-workout (training days only) |
| **Electrolytes** | Mid-shift if sweating a lot at Amazon | Pre-run |

**Caffeine math:** half-life 5-6 hours. If you sleep at 12:30 PM, last caffeine should be 6:30 AM at the latest. Earlier is better. After 6 AM, switch to caffeine-free fluids.

## Energy balance on shift days

Your TDEE on shift days is HIGHER than on off days because Amazon shift = 10 hours of physical work. Rough estimate:

- **Off day TDEE:** ~2,000-2,300 kcal (sedentary baseline + light workout)
- **Shift day TDEE:** ~2,500-2,800 kcal (BMR + 400-700 from shift + 200-300 from workout)

So on shift days you can eat ~300-500 MORE kcal than off days while still being in a fat-loss deficit. Your "deficit" target should still be ~400 kcal off TDEE for ~0.5 kg/week loss:

- **Off day eat:** ~1,800-2,000 kcal
- **Shift day eat:** ~2,100-2,400 kcal

The energy dashboard in the app handles this automatically once it pulls active calories from Health Connect.

## When the schedule breaks

Real life: you're going to have weeks where you miss workouts, eat at random times, sleep 4 hours. That's fine. Three principles:

1. **The 7-day average is what matters.** A bad shift day doesn't undo a good week.
2. **Never sacrifice sleep for a workout.** If you're at 5 hours of sleep, skip the workout. Period.
3. **Off-day intensity is your reset button.** A solid 60-min session on Friday off-day undoes a lot of mid-week chaos.

## Red flags (talk to a doctor)

Shift work is a documented health risk factor at the population level. Watch for:

- Resting HR creeping up >10 bpm above your normal
- Sleep < 5 hours for 3+ consecutive days
- Mid-shift fainting/dizziness
- New persistent digestive issues
- Mood significantly off (depression, irritability that doesn't fade)
- Persistent post-shift headaches

Most of these are manageable. None should be ignored.
